Our Story

"We didn’t come to sell chocolate. We came to say the things you couldn’t."

01

Life doesn’t come with subtitles.

No one hands you a script. No one tells you what to feel — or when.

02

Sometimes you can’t say sorry.

The words exist. They just refuse to leave your mouth at the right moment.

03

Sometimes you can’t say I love you.

Even when every part of you means it. Even when they’re right there.

04

Sometimes you just need comfort.

Not advice. Not solutions. Just something warm that says — I’m here.

— That’s Where Velvet Steps In —

05

Velvet understands before words do.

Every bar built around a feeling — not just a flavour. Real fruit. Real cocoa. Real emotion.

06

It melts like emotions.

Slowly. Completely. Without warning — exactly like the feelings we carry but never voice.

07

It comforts like a hug.

The kind that doesn’t need a reason. The kind that just holds.

08

It speaks when you can’t.

Pass a bar across the table. No explanation needed. They’ll understand.

09. Velvet is not eaten.

It is not just a chocolate. It is not just a snack. It is an act.

10. Velvet is felt.

And that — is everything we stand for.

About Velvet

We created a feeling.

Students across India sit in classrooms carrying worlds of emotion — pressure, loneliness, ambition, unspoken love — and the only chocolate available was cheap, forgettable, or made for someone else. Velvet was born to fix that.

Every bar contains real fruit pieces inside and real fruit blended into the chocolate. Not flavouring. Not syrup. The actual thing. No shortcuts. Ever.

Our Founder

The rebel who refused to wait.

Siddharth Rana

Founder · Velvet & Fruits Company · Gaya, Bihar · Age 22

“ I didn’t start Velvet to sell chocolate. I started it because I walked into classrooms full of brilliant students carrying silent battles — and I thought, someone should say something. So I did. With a chocolate bar. — Siddharth Rana

Siddharth was 22 when he noticed something everyone else had ignored. Coaching centres across India were packed with students who were stressed, lonely, and surrounded by nothing that truly spoke to them. Nobody made something for a 17-year-old sitting alone at 11pm, trying to crack JEE. So he decided to.

What He Actually Believes

1

Selling is secondary.

If you walk up to a student and your first instinct is to pitch — you’ve already lost. Ask why they’re sitting alone first. The bar comes last.

2

Motivation before marketing.

A student who believes in themselves will remember the person who believed in them first — and what was in their hand when it happened.

3

Success is not a dream.

It’s a decision you make at 2am when everyone else is asleep. You hustle, you ache, you rise — and you make every person who believed in you worth it.

4

Make your bloodline proud.

Not your batch topper. Not your teacher. The people who came before you worked so you could sit in that classroom. They deserve your best.

V
A Letter · To every student sitting alone right now

If you’re reading this sitting alone somewhere — I see you.

I won’t tell you it gets easier. I’ll ask you what’s making it hard. I’ll stay long enough to know your name, your dream, and what’s standing between you and it.

I won’t motivate you with posters or slogans. I’ll sit next to you and ask — what happened? What are you fighting?

And when you’ve said it all — only then will I say this:

"Success is not something you sleep and dream. You hustle. You ache. You fight for your bloodline, for everyone who worked so you could be in that room. And you rise."

Try Velvet. Not because it’s just a chocolate — but because every time you unwrap it, I want you to remember: someone out there built this for you. Someone who asked “why” before they said “buy”.

Siddharth Rana

Founder, Velvet · Real Fruit. Real Cocoa. Real Feeling.
